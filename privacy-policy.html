FocusFlow Privacy Policy

Last updated: December 2024

Overview

FocusFlow is a browser extension designed to help users stay focused by blocking distracting websites during focus sessions. This privacy policy explains how we handle your data.

Data Collection

We do not collect, store, or transmit any personal data. FocusFlow operates entirely on your local device.

Data Storage

FocusFlow stores the following information locally on your device:

  • Blocked websites list: The websites you choose to block during focus sessions
  • Focus settings: Your preferred focus duration and theme settings
  • Focus statistics: Local tracking of your focus sessions (optional)

All this data is stored locally in your browser's storage and is never sent to external servers.

Website Blocking

FocusFlow blocks websites by intercepting web requests to sites you've specified. This is done locally on your device and no information about your browsing activity is collected or transmitted.

Third-Party Services

FocusFlow does not use any third-party analytics, tracking, or data collection services.

Data Sharing

We do not share, sell, or transmit any of your data to third parties. All data remains on your local device.

Data Deletion

You can delete all FocusFlow data at any time by:

  • Uninstalling the extension
  • Clearing your browser's extension storage
  • Resetting the extension settings

Children's Privacy

FocusFlow is not intended for use by children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time. We will notify users of any material changes by updating the extension.

Your Rights

Since we don't collect personal data, there's no personal information to access, modify, or delete. All your data is stored locally and under your control.
